Pragathi Praveena

I design, build, and evaluate human-robot interfaces to enable remote collaborative work between people. I am currently a Ph.D. candidate in CS at the University of Wisconsin-Madison and I am advised by [Dr. Bilge Mutlu] (http://bilgemutlu.com/ ) and [Dr. Michael Gleicher.] (https://gleicher.sites.cs.wisc.edu/ )

Get a glimpse of my current research in this [1-minute teaser!] (https://twitter.com/HRIPioneers/status/1626663545810354176?s=20 ) I am most excited about the [Periscope robotic camera system] (https://www.researchgate.net/publication/370602657_Periscope_A_Robotic_Camera_System_to_Support_Remote_Physical_Collaboration ) which facilitates remote collaboration between two people. I have also been exploring the use of [collaborative robot arms in cinematography.] (https://dl.acm.org/doi/10.1145/3544549.3585715 )

I am on the job market for post-doc/research scientist/faculty positions! I prototype robotic systems (especially with cobots and cameras) and study user interactions with them (through mixed methods). I enjoy mentoring and put effort into fostering collective growth. I am deeply passionate about human collaboration, both in my research and outside.
